A short, woefully-incomplete list of the accomplishments of Herbert Hoover

"There is little importance to men's lives except the accomplishments they leave to posterity. When all is said and done, record of accomplishments is all that counts." —Herbert Hoover, 1916 [Whyte (2017), p. 176]

Hover your mouse over each accomplishment to see sources for it.

  • enrolled in Stanford University as its first-ever student
    • source 1
    • source 2
  • organized the first annual Big Game between Stanford and UC Berkeley

  • [1914-1919] distributed $865 million of food to >9 million people (over $20 billion in today's dollars), with only 0.46% overhead, in the geographical middle of WWI
    • on total cost and overhead: Whyte, "Herbert Hoover" (2017), p. 174
    • on number of people served: Whyte, "Herbert Hoover" (2017), p. 163
  • [1915] was offered a British citizenship, peerage, and cabinet post to run all British munitions production in WWI; turned it down without hesitation
    • Whyte, "Herbert Hoover" (2017), p. 173
  • [1917-1918] had dictatorial authority over all food-related industries in America during WWI; actually turned a profit with his agency's appropriation and working capital
    • on authoritative means: Whyte, "Herbert Hoover" (2017), p. 199
    • on returning a surplus: Whyte, "Herbert Hoover" (2017), p. 212
  • [1918-1920] had dictatorial authority over the entire European economy after WWI; managed to effectively stabilize it while preventing any significant famine
    • on authority: Whyte, "Herbert Hoover" (2017), p. 216

  • [1920] won the Democratic Party presidential primary in Michigan
  • [1928] elected U.S. President
